## Cosmere Books & Lore
-   **Warbreaker:** Is a strong contender for Cova's favorite singular novel due to its standalone nature and importance within the Cosmere.
-   **Mistborn (Era 1):** Recommends *The Final Empire* as a good starting point for the Cosmere, noting that *Hero of Ages* is particularly well-liked.
-   **Mistborn (Era 2):** Believes *The Lost Metal* requires prior Cosmere reading. Loves the magic system in Era 2, finding the new metals and combinations (like Wax's Steel Allomancy and Iron Ferruchemy) to be a "neat spin on Cosmere mechanics." Thinks Wayne becomes a "fairly well written character" by the end.
-   **Elantris:** Likes *Elantris* a lot, noting it has interesting cameos and presents another take on a world with a dead Shard.
-   **Stormlight Archive:** Recommends reading *Warbreaker* before *Words of Radiance* and strongly before *Oathbringer* for important exposition. Found *Oathbringer* to be "hype af." Considers Lopen and Lift "kinda silly."
-   **Secret Projects:** Liked *Tress of the Emerald Sea* for its "bedtime story vibe," but finds *Yumi and the Nightmare Painter* to have a more similar tone to other Cosmere books.
-   **YA Classification:** Believes *Mistborn* is technically YA but is also "borderline grimdark."
-   **Audiobook Narrators:** Michael Kramer and Kate Reading are the definitive narrators for almost all Cosmere books, with others being less preferred for standard audiobooks.

## MMO UI
-   **Clutter vs. Minimalism:** Considers most MMO UI screenshots to be cluttered, regardless of player choice. Prefers a minimalistic UI and finds excessive "radar doohickeys and excel graphs" to be too much, personally preferring "colored squares."

## Dungeons & Dragons / TTRPGs
-   **Time Abstraction:** Leans against mechanics that make travel time effectively meaningless, as it can negate other time-sensitive elements in the game. Would consider it only if it were not an everyday option and for very specific, rapid travel needs.
-   **RP Focus:** Values role-play heavy sessions and considers them important enough that attendance should be prioritized.
